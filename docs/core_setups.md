# CryptKeep Core Setups

## 1) Pullback Recovery
Thesis:
- Strong assets often recover after temporary dips.

Features:
- Relative strength rank
- Recent momentum
- Drawdown from recent high
- Volume vs average
- Liquidity / spread quality

Entry:
- Asset remains strong on medium lookback
- Short-term dip without structural breakdown
- Supportive liquidity / risk filters

Exit:
- First rebound target
- Momentum failure
- Time stop

Invalidation:
- Breakdown instead of pullback
- Weak liquidity
- Regime turns hostile

## 2) Low-Volatility Mean Reversion
Thesis:
- In compressed low-volatility conditions, oversold moves can revert toward the mean.

Features:
- RSI
- Distance from SMA
- Volatility regime
- Trend efficiency
- Volume ratio

Entry:
- Low-volatility regime
- Oversold condition
- Price below mean without breakdown

Exit:
- Reversion back toward mean
- Loss of setup quality
- Time stop

Invalidation:
- Volatility expansion against position
- Breakdown with poor follow-through

## 3) Momentum Continuation
Thesis:
- Strong leaders can continue higher after shallow consolidation.

Features:
- Relative strength persistence
- Multi-window returns
- Volume expansion
- Breakout confirmation
- Market regime

Entry:
- Strong trend
- Consolidation / shallow pullback
- Continuation confirmation

Exit:
- Trend weakness
- Rank deterioration
- Trailing stop / target logic

Invalidation:
- Failed breakout
- Volume collapse
- Adverse regime shift
